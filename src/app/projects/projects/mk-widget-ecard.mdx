## Overview

**MK Widget Card** is a digital business card app built to make modern networking feel faster, cleaner, and more memorable. Instead of carrying a printed card or typing contact details manually, users can create a polished eCard, show a QR code, scan another card, and save the result directly to their phone.

The project is special to me because it combines product design, mobile engineering, native iOS widget work, QR standards, and AI-assisted development into one complete app. It is not just a demo screen: it is a full product with a real App Store release, a clear use case, and a strong privacy angle.

The product website is available at <SmartLink href="https://mktechvn.com">mktechvn.com</SmartLink>. I use it as the public home for the MKTech ecosystem, where visitors can understand the app, explore the brand, and move from portfolio discovery to the real product experience.

## What The App Does

- **Digital eCard profile**: Create a clean contact card with name, role, company, phone, email, and personal branding.
- **vCard QR generation**: Generate a standards-compatible QR code so other people can save the contact immediately.
- **VietQR payment profile**: Store bank information and create a payment QR for faster personal or business transactions.
- **Smart QR scanner**: Scan an eCard QR, preview the parsed contact data, and save it to the phone contacts.
- **iPad presentation layout**: Use a larger, more premium layout for events, meetings, booths, or desk-side sharing.
- **Home-screen widget direction**: Designed around quick access, so the eCard can become part of the user's everyday device surface.

## Technologies Used

- **React Native** for the main cross-platform mobile app experience.
- **Swift / SwiftUI** for native iOS widget and platform-specific work.
- **Supabase** for backend storage, profile sync, and product data foundations.
- **QR and vCard standards** for contact generation, scanning, and device compatibility.
- **VietQR flow** for bank QR creation tailored to Vietnamese users.
- **AI-assisted engineering** for planning, prototyping, implementation, debugging, and iteration.

## AI-First Development

This project was built with an AI-first workflow from idea to release. I used AI to accelerate architecture planning, generate implementation options, compare mobile patterns, debug native integration issues, and refine UI details. My role was to direct the product, make engineering decisions, validate output, and connect every generated piece into a coherent app.

The result is a practical example of how AI can support a solo developer: faster exploration, broader technical reach, and more time spent on product decisions instead of repetitive scaffolding.

## Challenges and Learnings

The biggest challenge was making the app feel simple while supporting several different jobs: contact QR, banking QR, QR scanning, contact saving, iPad layout, and widget-ready thinking. I had to keep the main flow clean enough for non-technical users while still preserving flexibility for professional use.

Another major learning was balancing React Native speed with native iOS expectations. Some features need to feel close to the operating system, so the app required careful attention to layout rhythm, touch targets, visual contrast, and the handoff between JavaScript and native behavior.

## Outcome

**MK Widget Card** is now live on the **Apple App Store** and serves as one of my strongest portfolio projects. It demonstrates mobile product thinking, UI polish, QR-based utility, native platform work, and the ability to ship a full product with an AI-accelerated development process.
